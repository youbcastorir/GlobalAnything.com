Arabic Pronunciation Guide

Click on any letter to hear how it sounds (phonetic description)

ع
Ayn

A deep pharyngeal consonant unique to Arabic. No English equivalent.

غ
Ghayn

Like the French "r" — a voiced uvular fricative.

ح
Ha

A strong "h" from the throat — distinct from the softer ه.

خ
Kha

Like the "ch" in Scottish "loch" — a voiceless velar fricative.

ص
Sad

Emphatic "S" — pronounced with the tongue touching the roof of the mouth.

ق
Qaf

A "k" sound produced at the back of the mouth — distinct from ك.
